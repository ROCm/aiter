Two regimes: memory-bound → compute-bound
Small batches sit left of the ridge (HBM-bound: high MBU, tiny MFU because expert-weight loads dominate). As tokens grow, arithmetic intensity passes the ridge and the kernels turn compute-bound — MFU climbs to a plateau while MBU collapses. FP4: peak MBU 71.5% (t=64), plateau MFU 38.2% (t=65,536); INT8: peak MBU 78.1% (t=64), plateau MFU 44.6% (t=65,536).
FP4 vs INT8
At the top end (t=65,536) FP4 delivers 2,164 TFLOPS vs INT8 1,590 TFLOPS — about 1.4× faster, and moves half the weight bytes (0.5 B/elem vs 1 B/elem), so it stays bandwidth-efficient longer.
